Who we are

Built at the intersection of
medicine and data science

MediXtract was founded on the conviction that healthcare institutions are sitting on vast amounts of underutilised clinical knowledge — and that the right technology, developed in genuine partnership with clinicians, can unlock it.

"Transforming unstructured clinical data into the foundation of tomorrow's medical research."

We are a team of a biologist and a mathematical engineer dedicated to making healthcare data actionable for science. Our work sits at the boundary between technology and medicine — and that boundary is exactly where we operate best.

We don't build generic AI tools. Every system we create is developed in close collaboration with the clinical experts who will use it — shaped by the realities of clinical documentation, the needs of medical research, and the standards of responsible data governance.

Our north star is simple: every registry we help build should be something a researcher can trust, a clinician can understand, and an institution can be proud of.

MediXtract began with a frustration shared by clinicians across European academic medical centres: enormous amounts of valuable clinical data locked inside unstructured documents, inaccessible to research without years of manual abstraction work.

We started with a simple question: what if building a research-ready registry could be largely automated — without sacrificing the clinical precision that makes registry data valuable?

Our first answer came through the NEC registry project at Inselspital Bern, where we partnered directly with neonatologists to build the extraction and validation pipeline from the ground up — travelling to Bern in 2025 to meet the team in person and align on research objectives for the long term.

What we stand for

01
Clinical Grounding

Every product decision is shaped by people who understand the clinical context. We build with the institutions and experts who live the problems we're solving.

02
Radical Transparency

Our AI systems are never black boxes. Every extraction is traceable to its source. Every performance metric is visible. Institutions always know exactly how their data is being processed.

03
Responsible Innovation

We operate under the principles of Responsible Research and Innovation — involving the right stakeholders early, evaluating impact honestly, and never prioritising speed over safety.

04
Continuous Improvement

Deployment is not the finish line. We monitor, evaluate, and iterate. Our systems get better with every case processed and every piece of expert feedback received.

05
Data Culture Transformation

We aim to change how institutions think about their data — not just what they collect, but why, how it's structured, and what it can enable for future research.

06
Research-First Design

Everything we build is optimised for one end goal: registry data that meets the standards of rigorous medical research — complete, valid, traceable, and reproducible.
