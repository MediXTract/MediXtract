How we work

A methodology built for clinical reality

Every MediXtract project follows the same proven structure — iterative, transparent, and designed around the realities of clinical documentation and medical expert time.

We build intelligent data collection systems and evaluate their performance at scale.

Our methodology is not a one-size-fits-all deployment. It is a structured partnership — one that begins with deep engagement with the clinical team, iterates through cycles of tuning and feedback, and continues in production through ongoing monitoring and maintenance.

Each project is different. But the process is always the same: start with the clinical question, co-design the extraction logic with the experts who understand it, measure performance rigorously, and improve continuously.

The project lifecycle

Phase 01 Preliminary Study
Understanding the clinical landscape before writing a single line of extraction logic.

We begin by working directly with clinical specialists to understand the dataset — its structure, its coding practices, its terminology, and the clinical reasoning behind each variable. New variables are categorised and classified to determine the optimal technical approach for extraction. This phase sets the foundation for everything that follows.

Classification of variables into technical extraction categories
Definition of decision tree logic with clinical specialists
Identification of documentation patterns and edge cases
Adaptable to new clinical contexts — ~1 week per 100 variables with continuous specialist access
Phase 02 Extraction Engine Tuning
Iterative refinement until AI outputs match clinical ground truth.

MediXtract outputs are compared against human-validated data variable by variable. Discrepancies are classified — improvements (filled blanks, corrections, standardisations) versus issues requiring logic refinement (missing documentation, contradictions, ambiguous definitions, structural logic problems). The extraction engine is updated with each round until performance meets threshold.

AI vs human comparison for each variable in the batch
Prompt engineering and decision tree refinement based on discrepancies
Consistency testing (n=10) to identify ambiguous variable definitions
Chain of Thought reasoning audit to distinguish logic errors from fact errors
Phase 03 Evaluation & Report
Transparent performance metrics that institutions and research partners can trust.

We generate a structured performance report documenting precision and recall per variable, consistency scores, the distribution of discrepancy types, and any structural challenges identified in the dataset. This report is not just a deliverable — it is the input for the next phase, giving clinical experts exactly what they need to provide targeted feedback.

Precision and recall benchmarked per variable
Consistency scores flagging potentially ambiguous definitions
Discrepancy breakdown: improvements vs issues
Documentation of structural challenges for institutional awareness
Phase 04 Expert Feedback Integration
Closing the loop between AI performance and clinical knowledge.

Collaborative review sessions with medical professionals resolve clinical ambiguities, approve the final extraction logic for the cycle, and validate results from the previous patient cohort. This phase directly shapes the next cycle's preliminary study — creating a compounding improvement loop that gets tighter with every iteration.

Collaborative sessions with domain experts via the Schema Field Editor
Resolution of clinical ambiguities in variable definitions
Approval of extraction logic for production deployment
Validation of results from previous patient cohort

Principles that guide us

01
Co-development with experts

Clinical specialists are not consultants at the end of the process. They are co-designers from day one — shaping the decision trees, validating the reasoning chains, and approving the final logic before anything goes to production.

02
Continuous evaluation

We don't measure performance once at launch. Every cycle generates a new performance report. Every correction feeds back into the system. Accuracy improves as the clinical dataset grows.

03
Transforming data culture

We aim to change the way institutions think about registry design — not just automating what already exists, but helping clinical teams build better variables from the start, with clearer definitions and stronger research validity.

04
Privacy by design

Data governance is not an afterthought. Manual pseudoanonymisation, automated DLP scanning, regional data residency in Zurich, and AES-256 encryption are built into the pipeline from the first record processed.
