The Manual Entry Crisis

Current research is crippled by a systemic manual entry crisis.

Current retrospective research suffers from massive systemic inefficiencies that place immense pressure on medical professionals. Specialized staff are frequently trapped in "monolithic non-value work"—repetitive and exhaustive manual data abstraction tasks that lead to fatigue-driven errors and significant clinician burnout. In our pilot studies across major University Hospitals, we identified that one in five manually entered data points was objectively incorrect, while another 10% contained clinical ambiguities that threatened the reliability of research conclusions and patient care decisions.

The Collaboration Roadmap

Constant iterative cycles to reach total dataset stability.

We engage with institutions through constant iterative cycles designed to reach total dataset stability and long-term reliability. Each "circle" consists of four rigorous phases: a Preliminary Study to map your clinical variables, Extraction Engine Tuning to calibrate the AI against your hospital's ground truth, a comprehensive Evaluation & Audit report, and final Feedback & Approval from your medical experts. Remarkably, after the completion of the very first circle, the platform is already fully operational, allowing institutions to immediately start extracting and auditing historical data with high-precision results.
