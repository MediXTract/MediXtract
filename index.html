MediXtract is a clinical data extraction tool developed to streamline the review and structuring of neonatal medical records. The system is conceived not as a generic, one-size-fits-all program, but as a customized service that combines automated data extraction with an ergonomic user interface. This interface provides reviewers with the necessary clinical context to ensure that structured data entry remains both accurate and reproducible. In doing so, MediXtract prioritizes clarity, completeness, and traceability, while also supporting error detection and the synthesis of scattered information across medical records.

The tool is built on the principle that successful automation in medical documentation depends not only on computational performance, but also on a careful understanding of each dataset's structure and clinical relevance. By integrating this knowledge into both the extraction logic and the reviewer-facing interface, MediXtract ensures that automation does not replace expert judgment but rather enhances it, allowing clinicians and researchers to work more efficiently with complex neonatal datasets.

Features

MediXtract accelerates and brings reliability to the process of converting scattered clinical information into structured data. It achieves this through:

The design of a customized extraction system, based on feedback from specialists

Fields are grouped according to preprocessing needs, detailed extraction instructions are drafted, and tailored methods are developed to capture clinical context in complex scenarios. These methodological choices, however, cannot be made in isolation.

Close collaboration with medical experts is therefore crucial, as many questions arise regarding the correct classification of borderline cases and the intended scope of specific fields.

Through this iterative process:

  • We learn and adapt continuously from expert feedback
  • Refine prompts and models to improve accuracy
  • Gradually reduce the need for manual review

An ergonomic interface for human reviewers, incorporating clinical context and data quality alerts

Even when automated extraction reaches high levels of accuracy and is validated against historical and ongoing data, expert review remains indispensable before integrating new records into the dataset.

To support this stage, we have created a dedicated user interface that streamlines the review process. Reviewers receive the automated output together with contextual information, alerts for contradictions or ambiguities, a summary of clinically relevant details, and a quality analysis of the extracted records.

  • Users can review low-confidence or flagged outputs
  • Provide corrections that are stored as new training examples
  • Build a feedback loop for ongoing system enhancement

Structured reports providing methodological support for research

Retrospective studies often rely on datasets that were not originally designed for scientific purposes. This leads to inconsistent data quality, ambiguous field descriptions, and structural limitations in dataset design. Such shortcomings not only complicate analysis but also undermine the reliability of results, making systematic methodological support essential.

That is why, in addition to the extraction system and the reviewer interface, we produce structured reports specifically designed to support research. These reports highlight:

  • Data quality issues in previous registries
  • Ambiguous descriptions requiring clarification
  • Documentation gaps, contradictions, or inconsistencies
  • Strategies for restructuring specific fields in a simple and automated way

The problem with retrospective studies

Retrospective studies rely on pre-existing data sources â€“ such as electronic health records, surgical logs, or patient questionnaires â€“ that were not originally designed for scientific purposes. As a result, considerable effort is required for data cleaning, standardization, and validation. For large datasets, this process often involves many individuals manually entering structured data into registries, which introduces high error rates and inconsistent quality.1 The risk is further amplified by the fact that data entry often takes place outside normal working hours, under conditions of fatigue.

From problem to solution

MediXtract addresses these challenges by providing a unified methodological framework: all fields are processed consistently, following the same extraction rules. Moreover, we have developed tools that automatically detect ambiguous descriptions or poorly documented fields, allowing for systematic resolution rather than ad hoc corrections.

Our concern goes beyond simply ensuring that the data entered into the registry is correct â€“ we also aim to provide guarantees that this is verifiably the case. For this reason, an essential part of our methodology is the production of detailed documentation that systematically identifies and addresses: quality issues in previous registries, documentation gaps, structural problems in dataset design, and ambiguous descriptions requiring clarification.1

To make this information as accessible as possible, we complement technical reports with a dataset dictionary search tool that goes far beyond basic field descriptions. It provides insights into the extraction process itself, enriched with notes and explanations, all within a user-friendly and customizable interface.

In this way, we ensure reproducibility and transparency in how the data has been collected, which has a direct impact on the quality of research.

The NEC registry as our first use case

Necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC) is a severe gastrointestinal disease that primarily affects preterm infants. It is one of the leading causes of morbidity and mortality in this population, and survivors often face long-term neurodevelopmental and gastrointestinal complications. Despite decades of research, the pathophysiology of NEC and the factors driving disease severity remain incompletely understood, with retrospective studies highlighting heterogeneous risk factors such as low Apgar scores, congenital heart disease, and abnormal laboratory values.2

Our work so far has focused on validating MediXtract against the NEC minimal dataset through a detailed comparison of automated extractions with existing manually curated records. We analyzed 159 variables spanning birth data, radiological findings, medication use, surgical interventions, and temporal calculations. By applying our methodology to neonatal records, we were able to systematically identify discrepancies in manually entered data, highlight ambiguous or incomplete documentation, and suggest clinically consistent corrections. This first implementation demonstrates how our framework can not only support more reliable NEC registries, but also set a methodological precedent for tackling similar challenges in other clinical domains.

We are currently preparing a publication to showcase these results and make them available to the broader research community.

Development Roadmap

Our strategic plan for building and evolving the MediXtract system.

Phase 0

Meeting & Scope Alignment

We begin with an alignment meeting to set goals, timelines, and success metrics. Together, we decide how often you'll receive progress reports and define exactly what outcomes are expected. No dataset work is performed yet, this phase ensures a clear, shared foundation.

We start with an introductory meeting to align on scope, timelines, and goals. At this stage, we define which dataset will be adapted and clarify the outcomes that are expected. Success metrics are agreed upon in advance, including accuracy, reproducibility, and automation targets. Together, we also set the periodicity of reports and updates: our current capacity allows us to process around 100 variables per week per project, but the reporting cycle can be adapted to match your preferred pace, whether bi-weekly or monthly.

No dataset work takes place yet, this phase is dedicated exclusively to alignment and planning.

Phase 1

Dataset Review, First Extraction & Blueprint Report

We process the first batch of variables and deliver a comprehensive report. This report proposes dataset improvements, outlines the extraction blueprint, and assesses data quality. From this point, you can already start using the tool, with high-confidence fields automated and others available for expert review.

The first deliverable is a comprehensive report, built from the first batch of variables processed. It contains three main components:

  • Structural Review: Proposals to make the dataset clearer and more structured, including splitting variables for better coverage, merging or removing redundant ones, and clarifying ambiguous field descriptions.
  • Extraction Blueprint: The initial design of the extraction engine, including preprocessing steps, tailored methods to capture clinical context, and detailed instructions for reliable automation.
  • Data Quality Assessment: Highlights of systematic issues observed in the first batch of variables, such as temporal inconsistencies, diagnostic ambiguities, missing values, or classification errors.

At the same time, the first batch of variables are classified into three groups:

  • Manual-entry: variables that cannot be extracted automatically or semi-automatically, and therefore always require full human input.
  • Double-check: variables where AI predictions showed inconsistencies. These are flagged in the report, with clarification requests for experts. Once feedback is received, they are expected to move into the high-confidence group in the next cycle.
  • High-confidence: variables validated as consistently accurate and safe for immediate automation.

From this point forward, the tool becomes operational: high-confidence variables run automatically, double-check variables appear in the validation tab, and manual-entry variables remain outside automation.

Phase 2

Iterative Expansion & Cumulative Reports

We integrate feedback, update the engine, and process a new batch of variables each cycle. Every report mirrors the first, but now includes corrections and performance results for all variables so far (batch x2, batch x3, and so on). Over time, fewer issues appear as past clarifications are permanently resolved.

With the foundation established, we enter a cycle of expansion and refinement. Each new report mirrors the format of the first but expands in scope:

  • Structural Review: Reassesses the dataset, confirming whether earlier proposals were implemented and highlighting new structural adjustments suggested by the latest variables.
  • Extraction Blueprint: Documents the updates made to the extraction engine, incorporating the clarifications and feedback received from the prior report.
  • Data Quality Assessment: Evaluates the full dataset processed so far (e.g., 200 variables in cycle two, 300 in cycle three), showing improvements achieved on previous fields and performance metrics for the new batch. It also lists new questions, though these tend to decrease over time.

Each report therefore includes both the corrections from the last cycle and the results of the new variables. This cumulative approach ensures that with every iteration, the dataset grows in size, the engine grows in accuracy, and the volume of unresolved issues shrinks.

Phase 3

Iterative Circles of Expansion & Stabilization

The process continues in cycles, each expanding automation while reducing manual work. With every circle, the manual-entry group remains minimal, the double-check group shrinks, and the high-confidence group grows. After the necessary cycles, all variables are processed and the dataset reaches stability.

The process continues in circles (weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly, depending on the chosen cadence). Each circle adds 100 new variables, integrates expert feedback, and produces a cumulative report covering the entire dataset processed to date.

Over successive circles:

  • The manual-entry group is reduced to the unavoidable minimum.
  • The double-check group shrinks as more variables are clarified and validated.
  • The high-confidence group grows steadily, absorbing the majority of fields into automation.

By the end of this phase, after the necessary circles, all variables will have been processed. The dataset reaches a stable state in which automation dominates, and only a small fraction of fields remain for manual entry or occasional validation.

Phase 4

Rotational Audit & Comprehensive Report

We close the project with a rotational audit to verify accuracy and transparency. A comprehensive performance report summarizes automation rates, residual manual-entry fields, and overall quality. This confirms the system is reliable, reproducible, and ready for long-term use.

Once all variables are processed, we perform a rotational audit to confirm system stability and dataset reliability.

  • Rotational Audit: A random sample (e.g., 20% of the dataset) is directly compared against the original patient history to ensure transparency and accuracy.
  • Comprehensive Performance Report: A full report is delivered summarizing final results: automation rates, residual manual-entry fields, performance metrics across the dataset, and documentation of all improvements made along the way.

This phase ensures that the pipeline has not only reached completion but has been thoroughly validated for long-term reproducibility and trust.

User Interface Design

A comprehensive overview of the MediXtract interface workflow, from authentication to database management.

1
Authentication Portal

Sign-in page for team members to access the system using their assigned organization emails and passwords. This gateway protects everything that follows; once authenticated, users are routed to their role-appropriate workspace.

2
User Portal (Dashboard)

Landing dashboard after login. User roles are already assigned to each team member, so available actions and data are tailored by role. The page provides a quick summary of your work (ongoing cases, completed cases, and the fields your team handles). From here you have two primary actions: Process a New Patient or View the Dataset/Database. A Recent Activity section lists projects you or the team worked on most recently for fast re-entry.

3
Patient Data Processing

Portal for submitting source documents for a patient record. You can upload or update files in common formatsâ€”TXT, PDF, and Wordâ€”which will be queued for analysis. This is the starting point for AI-assisted extraction.

4
Document Intake Summary

After a file is uploaded, the system shows a compact document summary: detected document type, title, and character count. It also provides an estimated processing time. A Processing Summary indicates how many variables/fields will be evaluated and derives an overall time estimate from that scope. When ready, you can start the AI Processing.

5
Results Dashboard

The Results Dashboard appears while processing is underway. A progress pop-up in the top-right lets you monitor how far along the AI is. At the same time, the manual input fields are already visible, so you can begin entering required data manually while the AI runs in the background.

6
AI Processing In Progress

Processing status shows roughly 50% complete. The dashboard remains interactiveâ€”manual fields can continue to be filled, so human input and AI extraction proceed in parallel to save time.

7
AI Processing Complete

Once processing finishes, the dashboard loads two AI-driven review groups into separate tabs: High-Confidence Extractions and To-Be-Double-Checked. Manual-required inputs are shown as confirmed (after review by the associate researcher). A previously unavailable control becomes active: the Critical Fields button.

8
High-Confidence Extractions

This tab lists fields the AI extracts consistently and without (or with almost no) inconsistencies across iterations. Because these values are highly stable, you can usually click the blue Accept All button with confidence. Each field has an information (i) iconâ€”hover to see field-level guidance. Inside each field card you see the AI-predicted value; from here you can accept it immediately or open it for review if you want to inspect details first. The Critical Fields button is also available from here to open the AI's focused summary.

9
Critical Fields Review

When you choose to Review on any field box, a right-side panel opens showing Critical Fieldsâ€”the AI-generated summary of the most complex or consequential fields. This view is designed to streamline reading and verification. A search bar lets you find terms across this AI summary so you can quickly validate specific details.

Future Enhancements:

  • We plan to provide a short explanation of the AI's reasoning for each answer, so the reviewer understands why the AI produced that particular output. This will increase transparency and confidence in the results.
  • When opening the Review panel, the system will also automatically redirect you to the relevant part of the original document where the information is located, saving time and ensuring reviewers always see context immediately.
10
Original Document Viewer

Alternatively, you can open the original document in a dedicated viewer. This view also includes a search bar, allowing you to cross-check the extracted values directly against source text. Together with the Critical Fields panel, this enables fast, precise verification from both synthesized and original contexts.

11
Validation Complete

After you've confirmed and validated every field (or corrected any that needed adjustment), a confirmation pop-up appears indicating that all fields are validated. You can then send the results to the dataset (i.e., commit the verified values to the central database).

12
Patient Database

A database viewâ€”accessible from the User Portalâ€”listing all patients analyzed by the AI. For each record you can view, edit, or download. Filters let you narrow by Hospital and by Status (e.g., still being edited, finished, delivered). You can also sort records by Date or Patient ID to quickly locate the cases you need.

Other tools: Access to the Dataset Dictionary

To support the comprehensive validation and quality assurance processes, we developed a specialized Schema Field Editor web application that serves as the dataset dictionary and field management system. This tool enables systematic review, editing, and maintenance of dataset field definitions, providing comprehensive search and filtering capabilities for managing complex medical datasets with consistent quality control.

Originally created as an analytical tool for dataset examination, we are now migrating this application to serve as a comprehensive dataset control center. The enhanced version will provide real-time monitoring of variable performance, detailed classification systems for different data types, metadata management for each field, and advanced analytics to track data quality metrics over time. This evolution will transform it from a static dictionary into a dynamic management platform that offers insights into variable usage patterns, identifies potential data inconsistencies, and provides automated recommendations for dataset optimization and standardization.
